Separation of concerns

If we were pressed to boil software engineering down to one principle, or to identify the most important one of its best practices, we might well choose separation of concerns. Its influence is seen at all levels of our craft, from the “highest,” as in the division of a back end into a distribution of single-purpose microservices, to the “lowest,” as in the splitting of an overlong function into smaller ones. The operation is always the same: some chunk of the system, sometimes a “confused” one, is broken down into pieces that are then logically composed. And because these components are now independent, they have become easier to test, easier to reason about, and easier to improve.
